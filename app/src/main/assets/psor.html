DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

  • Intertrigo
  • Actinic Keratosis
  • Bowen's Disease
  • Dyskeratosis Follicularis
  • Ichthyosis Congenita
  • Mycosis Fungoides
  • Squamous Cell Carcinoma

CAUSES

  • genetics
  • common triggers:
    • an injury to the skin (cut, scrape, insect bite, sunburn)
    • excessive alcohol drinking
    • smoking
    • stress
    • hormonal changes (puberty, menopause, etc.)

SYMPTOMS

  • begin as small, reddish flaky, crusty patches of skin covered with silvery scales
  • progress to bigger patches or plaques
  • ormally appear on elbows, knees, scalp and lower back, but can appear anywhere
  • not contagious

PREVENTIVE TREATMENT

  • Moisturizers: reduces itching and scaling
  • Avoiding known triggers to psoriasis flare-ups

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR

  • If you suspect that you may have psoriasis, see your doctor for an examination. Also, talk to your doctor if your psoriasis:
    • Causes you discomfort and pain
    • Makes performing routine tasks difficult
    • Causes you concern about the appearance of your skin
    • Leads to joint problems, such as pain, swelling or inability to perform daily tasks
