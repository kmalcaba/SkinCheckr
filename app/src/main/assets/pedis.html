DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

  • Allergic Contact Dermatitis, Acute & Chronic
  • Atopic Dermatitis
  • Dyshidrotic Eczema
  • Pustular Psoriasis of the Palms and Soles

CAUSES

  • Trichophyton rubrum, T. mentagrophytes, or Epidermophyton floccosum
  • walking barefoot in places where someone else has athlete's foot
  • touching the affected skin of someone with athlete's foot
  • more likely to acquire in a person with wet or sweaty feet, or if the skin on the feet is damaged

SYMPTOMS

  • involves the soles and interdigital spaces between the feet
  • intensely pruritic
  • itchy white patches between the toes
  • red, sore and flaky patchesr
  • skin that may crack and bleed

PREVENTIVE TREATMENT

  • Keep your feet clean and dry, particularly between the toes
  • Use a separate towel for your feet and wash it regularly
  • Wear clean socks
  • Do not scratch affected skin; it can spread to other parts of the body
  • Do not walk around barefoot
  • Do not share towels, socks or shoes with other people

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR

  • if you're in a lot of discomfort
  • if your foot is red, hot and painful; this could be a more serious infection
  • if you have diabetes; foot problems can be more serious with diabetes
  • if you have a weakened immune system
