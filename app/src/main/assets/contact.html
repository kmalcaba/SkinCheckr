DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

  • Atopic dermatitis
  • Tinea Corporis
  • Dyshidrotic eczema
  • Psoriasis
  • Scabies

CAUSES

  • Irritant Contact Dermatitis: It happens when the skin touches an irritating chemical, experiences too much friction, or makes contact with heat
  • Allergic Contact Dermatitis: caused by an allergic reaction
  • Occupational Contact Dermatitis: workers may come into repeated, frequent exposure to irritants or allergens, such as rubber, latex, or chemicals
  • Photocontact Dermatitis: occurs after a person comes into contact with an irritant or allergen and the area of contact subsequently receives sun exposure that causes a reaction

SYMPTOMS

  • Read rash
  • Itching, which may be severe
  • Dry, cracked, scaly skin
  • Bumps and blisters
  • Swelling, burning or tenderness
  • Skin that appears darkened or leathery
  • Sun sensitivity

SELF-CARE MEASURES

  • Avoid scratching your irritated skin which may even cause a skin infection that requires antibiotics
  • Clean your skin with mild soap and lukewarm water to remove any irritants
  • Cold compresses can help with the itch

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR

  • The rash is so uncomfortable that you are losing sleep or are distracted from your daily activities
  • The rash is sudden, painful, severe or widespread
  • The rash affects your face or genitals
