DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

  • Vitiligo
  • Erythrasma
  • Pityriasis Lichenoides Chronica
  • Seborrheic Dermatitis

CAUSES

  • proliferation of the lipophilic yeast, which is part of the normal flora of human skin
  • living in a warm, moist environment
  • sweating excessively
  • clothing that don't allow your skin to breathe
  • being malnourished, having a weak immune system

SYMPTOMS

  • chronic, noninflammatory, usually symptomless, not contagious, harmless
  • multiple macular patches of all sizes and shapes
  • varying in pigmentation from fawn-coloured to brown
  • can occur at any age but most commonly seen in young adults
  • areas most affected include the back, chest, upper arms, neck, and abdominal area

PREVENTIVE TREATMENT

  • Stop using skin care products that are oily
  • Wear loose clothes
  • Protect your skin from the sun
  • Do not use a tanning bed or sun lamp

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR

  • Your skin doesn't improve with self-care measures
  • The fungal infection returns
  • The patches cover large areas of your body
