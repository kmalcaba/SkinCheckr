DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

  • Tinea Pedis
  • Atopic dermatitis
  • Allergic Contact Dermatitis

CAUSES

  • A fungal skin infection: this may be on the hands or at a distant site from the blisters (such as in between the toes)
  • A reaction to something that has touched your skin such as certain metals (particularly nickel), detergents, household chemicals, soap, shampoo, cosmetic products or perfume
  • Stress: it appears to be more common during times of emotional or physical stress
  • Seasonal allergies
  • Staying in water for too long
  • Excessive sweating of the hands or feet

SYMPTOMS

  • Vesicular eruptions on the palms and soles, particularly along the sides and between the digits
  • Burning sensation
  • Excessive sweating
  • Excessive itching of the skin
  • typically symmetric, chronic relapsing course
  • involving mainly the face, neck and the flexural surface of the elbow and knee

PREVENTIVE TREATMENT

  • Consistently applying moisturizer soon after getting out of the shower or bath.
  • Wearing soft, loose clothing made of natural fibers, such as cotton.
  • Refraining from scratching or itching, as this can break the skin and worsen the condition
  • Avoid triggers that can cause eczema flare-upsg

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR

  • Call your doctor if you have a rash on your hands or feet that doesn't go away on its own (2-3 weeks)
  • Secondary bacterial infection which results in pain, swelling, and pustules
