DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

  • Benign mole
  • Seborrheic keratosis
  • Pigmented basal cell carcinoma
  • Pigmented actinic keratosis
  • Dermatofibroma

CAUSES

  • sudden intense exposure to ultraviolet light, sunbed exposure
  • certain characteristics can increase chances of melanoma, such as:
    • lots of moles or freckles
    • pale skin that burns easily
    • red or blonde hair
    • a close family member with a history of melanoma

SYMPTOMS

  • a new mole or a change in an existing mole
  • most commonly affected areas: back in men, legs in women
  • irregular shape and are more than one color, may be larger than normal
  • Bleeding
  • Itchy

PREVENTIVE TREATMENT

  • Avoid prolonged intense exposure to the sun
  • Protect yourself from sun damage by using sunscreen
  • Sunbeds and sunlamps should be avoided

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR

  • if you suspect to have melanoma or if a mole has changed in size/shape and/or has bled, consult a doctor immediately
